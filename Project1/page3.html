The economic ripple effect of the Kumbh Mela is profound, making it one of India’s most financially impactful religious events.

The 2025 Maha Kumbh Mela is not only a spiritual event but also a major economic driver, with an estimated budget of $800 million allocated for organization and infrastructure development.
The Mela creates thousands of temporary jobs, benefiting small businesses, hoteliers, transport providers, and local artisans. Street vendors selling religious artifacts, food, and souvenirs experience a surge in income, while transportation services see record-breaking demand.
The festival is expected to generate between $30 billion and $35 billion, significantly boosting the local and national economy.
Pilgrims also contribute to hospitality, tourism, and retail sectors, with many extending their stay beyond the festival to explore Prayagraj’s historical and religious sites.
Additionally, international visitors bring in foreign exchange revenue, further strengthening economic growth.
